The Appearance menu — five directions

Same player, same frame, same settings underneath. What changes is how the menu presents itself. Today's version is a white Mantine panel roughly 470 px tall; every one of these is under 200 px and speaks the player's own language (bar colour #12161d, brand purple, Tabler glyphs).

Direction 1
Drill-in

One short list. Each row shows its current value; tapping it swaps the panel for that group. The panel never grows — you go deeper, not longer. This is how YouTube, Netflix and every TV remote UI does it.

Direction 2
Live preset cards

The four presets stop being words and become pictures of themselves — each tile draws a miniature highlight at its own settings. You choose by looking, not by reading. Everything else hides behind one Fine-tune row.

1:30 / 5:32

Costs: four tiny renderers to build and keep truthful. Buys: a low-vision viewer picks their setting by sight — which is the entire product thesis.

Direction 3
On-canvas

Delete the panel. The highlight itself becomes editable: corner handles for size, a floating chip strip for thickness, colour and pointer. Nothing covers the video because there is no menu — you're touching the thing you're changing.

Direction 4
Icon rail

A 52 px column of Tabler glyphs on the left, one group's controls on the right. Everything stays one tap away — no drilling — but only one group is ever on screen, so the panel is fixed at 196 px.

Direction 5 · recommended
Cards, then drill

2 and 1 are not rivals — they are the two halves of one menu. The tiles are the front door, because 90% of people will pick one and leave. Fine-tune opens the same panel as a drill-in list, so the sheet never grows past its own height.
