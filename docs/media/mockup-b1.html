Auto-hiding panel · moments in the timeline · moments as a right sidebar

Auto-hide is safe here as long as the panel also reveals on keyboard focus, on pause, and on any [/] press — not on pointer movement alone. That is what makes it usable without a mouse.

Part 1 — where do the moments go in the timeline?
Three treatments, same six moments

Yellow = played · grey = analyzed · red = a moment · the pale bar is the analysis frontier.

T1 · Marker lane under the scrubber

Moments get their own row. The scrubber stays a scrubber.
Each is its own click target. Scrubbing never fights jumping. Costs ~19px of height.

T2 · Split track — moments in the bottom half

One thicker bar. Progress on top, moments underneath, fused.
Compact and reads as one object. But the two halves are 11px each — a click near a moment is ambiguous: scrub, or jump?

T3 · Moments drawn into the track

YouTube-chapter style. The moments live inside the scrubber itself.
Cheapest in space, but a moment under the playhead is invisible, and there is no separate thing to click or Tab to. The moments stop being navigable.
